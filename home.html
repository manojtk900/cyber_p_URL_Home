About Phishing — What you need to know

Phishing is a social‑engineering attack that tricks people into giving sensitive information. ASTRAL PROTECTION curates awareness videos, real case studies, legal guidance and reporting steps so communities can detect, report and recover from phishing attacks.

How to Protect Yourself

Verify sender
Check the email address — don't rely on display names. Hover over links to preview the destination URL.
Never share OTPs
Banks and legitimate services never ask for passwords or OTPs over chat or calls.
Enable 2FA
Use authenticator apps, keep software and browsers updated, and use password managers.

Acts, Laws & Reporting (India)

Information Technology Act, 2000 — Relevant sections
Sections include identity theft, cheating by personation, and unauthorized access.
Key sections used in phishing cases: Section 66C (identity theft), Section 66D (cheating by personation) and provisions under Section 43 for damage to computer systems. These sections can lead to fines and imprisonment depending on the offense.
RBI & Banking Guidelines
Banks must educate customers, report frauds and follow fraud risk-management directions.
Reserve Bank of India guidelines require banks to notify customers about cyber risks, track and report frauds, and provide a mechanism for redressal. Customers should inform their bank immediately on noticing unauthorised transactions.
How to report phishing / open a case
Practical reporting steps for victims and defenders.
1. Preserve evidence: Save the full email (headers), screenshots of the site, URLs and any messages. 2. Do not interact further with the attacker: avoid clicking links or replying. 3. Notify your bank / payment provider immediately if financial info was shared. 4. Report to CERT‑In (incident@cert-in.org.in) and use local police cyber cell or the national cybercrime portal. 5. Change passwords from a safe device and enable 2FA; scan devices for malware. 6. Share a short case summary with our project if you want us to document it for awareness (anonymised by default).
